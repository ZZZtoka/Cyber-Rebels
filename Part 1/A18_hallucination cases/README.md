## A18 – AI Hallucination

## Description
I explored hallucination cases in generative AI systems, where the AI produces incorrect or fabricated information.

## Findings
- The AI provided incorrect information confidently
- The AI generated details about a non-existent country (Wakanda)
- The AI created false historical facts and roles that are not real

## Evidence
Figure 1: AI incorrectly identifying a CEO of Google in 1995.
![ai](evidence/1.jpg)

Figure 2: AI generating false information about a fictional country.

## Analysis
AI hallucination occurs when a model produces false or fabricated information while appearing confident. In these examples, the AI generated incorrect facts and fictional details without indicating uncertainty. This demonstrates the limitations of generative AI systems and the risk of misinformation if users do not verify outputs. Critical thinking and fact-checking are essential when using AI tools.

## Reflection
This activity helped me understand that AI systems are not always reliable and that users must verify information from trusted sources.
