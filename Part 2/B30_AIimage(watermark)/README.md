## 30_ AI Watermarking and Hidden Image Analysis

## Description
I explored AI image watermarking and hidden watermark detection techniques using an AI-generated image and online steganography analysis tools. The activity focused on embedding a low-opacity watermark into an image and analysing whether the hidden watermark could still be detected.

## Findings
- AI-generated images can contain hidden or low-visibility watermarks
- Watermarks can be embedded using opacity and image layering techniques
- Steganography tools can reveal hidden image information
- Hidden watermarks may still be recoverable after image processing
- Watermarking helps verify ownership and authenticity of digital content

## Analysis
Digital watermarking is commonly used to protect ownership, authenticity, and traceability of digital images. In this activity, a low-opacity watermark was embedded into an AI-generated image and later analysed using StegOnline bit-plane analysis tools. By examining the image through different bit planes, hidden watermark information became visible even though it was difficult to notice in the original image. This demonstrates how image data can contain hidden information that may not be immediately visible to users. Watermarking and steganography techniques are widely used in cybersecurity, copyright protection, digital forensics, and AI-generated media verification.

## Reflection
This activity helped me understand how hidden information can be embedded inside digital images using watermarking and steganography concepts. I learned that even low-visibility modifications can sometimes be detected using image analysis tools and bit-plane extraction techniques. The activity also showed the importance of watermarking for protecting AI-generated content and verifying authenticity in digital media.
